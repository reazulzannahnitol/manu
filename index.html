Why Romania? Key Advantages for Foreign Investors

Romania offers a robust blend of economic stability, attractive tax benefits, and a thriving business environment, making it one of Europe’s prime locations for foreign investors.

  • Strategic EU Location with Eastern Access: Romania bridges Western and Eastern Europe, allowing companies to operate seamlessly across both markets, benefiting from the EU’s free-trade zone.
  • Competitive Corporate Taxation: Romania’s 16% flat corporate tax rate is one of the lowest in the EU, encouraging foreign investment. Additional tax incentives are available for R&D activities, IT companies, and industrial activities.
  • Extensive Skilled Workforce: Romania boasts a highly educated and multilingual workforce, particularly in IT, engineering, and manufacturing sectors, with competitive labor costs compared to Western Europe.
  • Dynamic Economy and Growing Consumer Market: With sustained GDP growth, Romania has one of the fastest-growing economies in Europe, providing a favorable market for both B2B and B2C companies.
  • EUFunding and Business Grants: Access to various European Union grants for sectors like tech, agriculture, green energy, and manufacturing, supporting the growth of innovative and sustainable businesses.
  • Double Tax Treaties: Romania has double tax treaties with over 80 countries, allowing foreign business owners to optimize their tax obligations by avoiding double taxation.
  • Attractive Real Estate Opportunities: With a burgeoning real estate market, investors find affordable options for offices, logistics centers, and production facilities compared to other European countries.

Legal and Fiscal Landscape in Romania

Overview of Romanian Fiscality for Foreign-Owned Businesses

Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

Romania applies a flat 16% corporate income tax (CIT) rate, making it one of the most competitive in the EU. Certain small businesses may qualify for a reduced rate under the micro-enterprise tax regime, applicable to companies with turnover below EUR 1 million.

Micro-Enterprise Tax System

Micro-enterprises, typically with a turnover below EUR 1 million, are taxed at rates as low as 1% (if they have employees) or 3% (if they don’t have employees). This system provides substantial benefits for smaller companies starting operations in Romania.

Value-Added Tax (VAT)

The standard VAT rate is 19%, with reduced rates of 9% for food and medicines, and 5% for select categories like housing. Foreign companies can register for VAT if their turnover exceeds EUR 88,500 or if engaging in EU transactions.

Withholding Tax

Romania applies a 5% withholding tax on dividends paid to foreign shareholders. However, this can be reduced under EU rules or eliminated if the receiving entity is based in a country with a tax treaty with Romania.

Tax Incentives for Innovation and R&D

Romania provides tax incentives for companies engaged in research and development, including additional deductions and exemptions, aimed at fostering growth in the tech and innovation sectors.

Social Contributions & Labor Taxes

Employers are required to make social security contributions on behalf of employees, totaling around 2.25%. Additionally, a 10% income tax is applied to employees’ salaries.

Double Tax Treaties & Avoidance

Romania has tax treaties with over 80 countries to prevent double taxation, offering favorable conditions for international investors.

Legal Requirements and Compliance Considerations

Corporate Governance

Romanian companies must comply with specific governance structures. For example, an SRL requires at least one director and shareholder, who can be the same individual.

Labor Law

Romania has a comprehensive labor law system covering employment contracts, minimum wage, working hours, and employee benefits. Our team can help ensure your contracts are compliant with local regulations.

Data Protection (GDPR Compliance)

Companies operating in Romania must comply with GDPR regulations to protect personal data. We provide GDPR guidance to ensure your business meets EU standards.

Accounting & Financial Reporting

All companies are required to file annual financial statements and maintain proper records in compliance with Romanian accounting standards. Our accounting team provides full support to keep your company compliant.

FAQ

What are the key advantages of registering a company in Romania? ▼

Romania offers several advantages for foreign investors, including a strategic EU location bridging Eastern and Western Europe, a competitive 16% flat corporate tax rate, a highly skilled workforce, and access to EU funding. Additionally, Romania’s growing economy and business-friendly environment make it an ideal destination for foreign investment.

What types of business structures can I register in Romania? ▼

In Romania, the most common business structures for foreign investors are the SRL (Limited Liability Company) and SA (Joint Stock Company). We assist in choosing the right structure for your business based on your investment goals and industry requirements.

What is the process for company registration in Romania? ▼

The company registration process in Romania involves several key steps: Initial consultation to select the best business structure, document preparation and notarization, fiscal and tax registration, filing with the National Trade Registry, business bank account setup, and obtaining necessary licenses or permits based on your business sector.

Are there tax incentives for innovation and R&D in Romania? ▼

Yes, Romania offers tax incentives for companies engaged in research and development (R&D). These include additional deductions and exemptions, designed to encourage investment in innovation and the growth of tech and R&D sectors in the country.

How long does it take to register a company in Romania? ▼

The process of company registration in Romania typically takes 5–10 business days, depending on the accuracy of the submitted documentation. However, additional time may be needed for permits and licenses based on your industry.

Do I need to be physically present in Romania to register a company? ▼

No, you do not need to be physically present in Romania to register a company. We provide full remote support, including document notarization and translation services, to guide you through the entire registration process from abroad.
